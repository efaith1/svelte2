Esther Kinyanjui - MIT 6.C35, Spring 2024

Proposition: The data on civilian complaints filed against the NYPD reveals systemic patterns of unequal treatment, particularly towards minority communities.

FOR the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Color Choice for Demographic Groups:
    Score: 1.5 (Earnest)
    Decision: Use a distinct color palette for each demographic group, with colors chosen to be easily distinguishable and visually appealing.
    Rationale: By using distinct and visually appealing colors for each demographic group, the chart becomes more engaging and easier to interpret. This decision helps users quickly identify the disproportionate number of complaints filed by minority communities compared to white individuals, supporting the proposition effectively.
  • Ordering of Complaint Categories:
    Score: 1 (Earnest)
    Decision: Arrange complaint categories in descending order of frequency within each demographic group.
    Rationale: By ordering complaint categories based on frequency, the chart emphasizes the most common issues reported within each demographic group. This reinforces the narrative of systemic issues within the NYPD, as certain categories of complaints may stand out more prominently, further supporting the proposition.
  • Textual Content: Clear Title and Annotations:
    Score: 2 (Earnest)
    Decision: Include a clear and descriptive title for the visualization, along with annotations highlighting key insights or disparities.
    Rationale: A clear title and informative annotations help guide viewers' attention to the main message of the visualization, reinforcing the proposition. By explicitly stating the purpose of the visualization and highlighting key findings, viewers are more likely to grasp the significance of the data and the implications for systemic issues within the NYPD.

AGAINST the Proposition

Design Decisions and Rationale:

  • Data Transformation: No Aggregation or Normalization:
    Score: -1.5 (Deceptive)
    Decision: Create a calculated field to give the number of resolved cases and display raw counts of complaint resolutions without aggregating or normalizing by population size.
    Rationale: Without aggregating or normalizing the complaint resolution counts, the visualization may inaccurately portray the resolution rates across racial groups. This decision could lead to misleading interpretations of the data and undermine the persuasiveness of the visualization in demonstrating equitable treatment by the NYPD.
  • Time Interval for Trend Analysis:
    Score: -1.5 (Deceptive)
    Decision: Use inconsistent time intervals for trend analysis, such as displaying data points without regular intervals.
    Rationale: Inconsistent time intervals make it difficult for viewers to accurately interpret trends over time and compare complaint resolutions across racial groups. This decision could weaken the persuasiveness of the visualization by introducing ambiguity and hindering viewers' ability to discern any meaningful patterns or disparities in complaint resolutions.
  • Color Choice for Race Segmentation:
    Score: -1 (Deceptive)
    Decision: Use the same color for all racial groups in the bar graph.
    Rationale: By using the same color for all racial groups, the visualization may obscure the differences between them, potentially downplaying any disparities in complaint resolutions. This decision could weaken the persuasiveness of the visualization by making it less clear and impactful in demonstrating equitable resolution of complaints across different racial groups.

Final Reflection

Balancing the need to effectively communicate a message with the responsibility to present data accurately and transparently required careful consideration at every step. While some design decisions were straightforward and aligned with ethical principles, such as providing clear titles and annotations, others presented dilemmas, particularly when it came to representing data in a visually compelling yet honest manner.

One aspect that surprised me was the impact of seemingly minor design choices on the persuasiveness of the visualizations. For example, the decision to use consistent color palettes and data normalization significantly influenced the clarity and credibility of the visual narratives. It highlighted the importance of attention to detail and critical thinking in ethical analysis and visualization.

I now define ethical analysis and visualization as the conscientious and deliberate process of presenting data and insights in a manner that respects the truthfulness, integrity, and dignity of the information and its stakeholders. It involves not only adhering to ethical standards and best practices but also continuously reflecting on the potential implications and consequences of design decisions on the audience and society at large.
